Bio- Medical Device Fabrication and Advanced Material Lab

Bio-DFAM Lab • SRM University Sikkim

Bio-DFAM Lab is a research group at SRM University Sikkim working at the intersection of biomedical device fabrication, advanced functional materials, sensing systems, and image-based analysis workflows for practical healthcare and diagnostic applications.

The lab combines materials science, chemistry, embedded prototyping, and digital analysis to build research outputs that are not only scientifically strong, but also suitable for real-world translation.

Research

Biomedical device fabrication

Development of practical biomedical devices and interfaces for sensing, testing, and applied healthcare use.

Advanced functional materials

Design and characterization of responsive materials for analytical, diagnostic, and translational research applications.

Colorimetric and VOC sensing

Investigation of sensing systems for acetone and related analytes using visible response, image quantification, and low-cost experimental workflows.

RGB and image-based analytics

Quantitative extraction of color shifts, ROI analysis, and grid-based mapping to transform photographed sensor responses into interpretable data.

Projects and Tools

Exposure RGB Mapper

A web-based tool for comparing before and after exposure images, selecting crop regions, measuring RGB shifts, and exporting structured sensor results.

Phone-assisted sensor analysis workflow

A lightweight image capture and analysis approach designed to make laboratory sensing experiments easier to quantify outside specialized imaging setups.
